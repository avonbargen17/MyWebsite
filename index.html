The Big Leagues

The Major League Baseball Organization is the oldest of the four most popular
sports in America and Canada. It consists of two leagues, the National and
American leagues, officially joined in 2000 by the Commissioner of Baseball
The MLB also oversees the Minor Leagues and hosts the World Baseball Classic.
The league originated in Cincinnati, 1869, where players had created rivalries
and started competing. The sport soared in popularity from the 1920's past
World War Two when Jackie Robinson joined the league, breaking the color barrier.
Now there are 30 teams which each play 160 regular season games competing to
qualify for the playoffs and win their way to the World Series.
